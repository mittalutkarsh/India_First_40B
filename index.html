The problem

Three goals, one hard constraint

Capability at 40B is not the hard part. The constraint is native Indic text: the largest cleaned corpus tops out near ~280B tokens against a 1.32T Indic budget. Repetition (up to four epochs, near-free) covers most of the gap; the rest is manufactured.

Manufactured text carries two risks: it fragments under English-centric tokenizers (high fertility), and it imports "translationese," the source language's framing. Native data keeps a documented 3-to-11-point edge on culturally-loaded tasks even after identical fine-tuning, and prompting in the local language does not remove a Western skew. So we will build native-first, tag every document by origin, and — the novel part — measure what provenance does to the model's internal "perspective direction."

What we will do

The plan, in one place

  • Data (native-first, provenance-tagged). Sort every Indic document into native-verified, native-web, native-synthetic, and translated tiers; repeat scarce native text up to four epochs; fill the rest with bottom-up synthetic and minimal translation. Order the reading so low-resource languages are not drowned out.
  • Cleaning (objective-driven). Family-aware language ID, conservative script normalization, perplexity filtering, deduplication, and — the critical stage — a translationese filter that keeps the native tiers actually native.
  • Tokenizer & fertility. A custom tokenizer that minimizes the worst language's fertility, not the average, with a 256K vocabulary and per-language targets capped at 2.5.
  • The research core. A contrastive perspective corpus and a set of metrics (F1–F7) that extract and measure the Indian-perspective direction, and test whether native provenance produces a cleaner, more durable one.
  • Post-training. Native-authored instruction tuning, then alignment where the Indian vantage is set — with a separate non-chauvinism check so the model can never win by demonising others.
In depth

Each section, explained

01Problem & Approach

A 40-billion-parameter model reaches Gemma-4-level capability without much difficulty; the binding constraint is native Indic text. The largest cleaned corpus, Sangraha plus IndicCorp, tops out near ~280B tokens against a 1.32T Indic budget. Repeating high-quality data up to about four epochs is nearly free in loss and recovers most of that gap, so the corpus need not be synthetic-heavy.

The danger is manufactured text: it fragments under English-centric tokenizers and carries translationese, the source language's framing, and native data holds a documented 3-to-11-point edge on culturally-loaded tasks even after identical fine-tuning. The novel move is to stop treating the perspective vector as a knob on a finished model and instead read its geometry as a signature of the training data, in a from-scratch setting where we control that data.

02Data Strategy

The 6T-token budget splits into English 40%, code 20%, Indic 22%, math and science 12%, and other multilingual 6%, with India-centric English routed into the English slice. Every Indic document is tagged by origin across four tiers: native-verified, native-web, native-synthetic, and translated.

A repetition rule — budget = epochs × unique-native + manufactured, with epochs capped near four — lets scarce native text carry most of the load, so about 85% of the Indic budget is native-provenance and 15% manufactured. Because low-resource languages get drowned out in a uniform from-scratch mix, the reading is phased: high-resource-heavy first, Indic ramped sharply next, and a final anneal on the highest-quality native text.

03Data Cleaning

Cleaning is objective-driven rather than generic hygiene, built on AI4Bharat's Setu. Family-aware language identification separates script-sharing languages such as Hindi and Marathi, conservative normalization canonicalizes encodings without breaking morphology, perplexity filtering removes low-quality text, and deduplication keeps the repetition accounting honest.

The critical stage is the translationese filter: machine-translated text shows lower type-token ratio and unigram entropy, and a document enters a native tier only if it passes, which is what keeps the native tiers native. Decontamination holds the contrastive perspective corpus out of pretraining, so the separability metric measures a learned vantage rather than memorization.

04Tokenizer & Fertility

Fertility, the number of tokens a word is split into, sets how much content a fixed budget yields: at fertility 6 the Indic budget is worth 220B words; at fertility 2 it is worth 660B. English-centric tokenizers fragment Indic scripts at 4–8 tokens per word, so the tokenizer will target 1.4–2.1.

The objective minimizes the worst language's fertility rather than the average, via parity-aware BPE with morphology-aware pre-tokenization for the Dravidian scripts, capping the worst case at 2.5. The vocabulary is 256K, about 4% of the model in embedding parameters, and fertility is tracked alongside parity, perplexity, type-token ratio, deduplication overlap, and bits-per-byte.

05Research Core

The contribution is a way to measure whether provenance shapes the model's vantage. We author contrastive pairs — the same prefix with one Indian-vantage and one Western-default continuation — and use contrastive surprisal (F1, F2) to locate the framing-bearing tokens, since perspective-bearing content is improbable under a Western-skewed model.

From these we extract a perspective direction (F3, and the surprisal-weighted F4), then measure its separability (F5), entanglement (F6), and persistence through alignment (F7). The headline test compares native against translated provenance on small proxy models, at matched capability, before committing to the full run.

06Post-training

After pretraining, native-authored instruction data, anchored on IndicAlign's ~74.7M pairs, turns the model into an instruction-following, tool-using agent, since native instruction tuning beats translated on generative tasks. Alignment is where the Indian vantage is set, through three streams: Indian-annotator preferences, a constitution derived from Indian constitutional principles, and in-language safety data.

Preference triples pair an Indian-vantage, factual answer against both a Western-default and a chauvinistic one, with the non-chauvinism axis scored separately so the model can never win by demonising others.
