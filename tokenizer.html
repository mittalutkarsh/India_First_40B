Tokenizer & Fertility

B.3 · Tokenizer and fertility

Tokenizer strategy: we will train a custom tokenizer that treats fertility as a first-class objective and enforces fairness across scripts.

Fertility, the number of subword tokens per word, governs how much linguistic content a fixed token budget yields:

effective words  =  token budget  /  fertility

English-centric tokenizers fragment Indic scripts at 4–8 tokens per word (over 10 on Telugu for Llama-3 and Qwen-2.5); the tokenizer will target 1.4–2.1.

We will minimize the worst-case fertility rather than the mean, so a low Hindi figure cannot be bought at the cost of a high Malayalam one:

standard objective:   minimize   (1/L) · Σ_ℓ fertility(ℓ)
our objective:       minimize   ( max_ℓ fertility(ℓ) )    (parity-aware BPE)

This will be parity-aware BPE (Foroutan et al., 2025), extended with morphology-aware pre-tokenization for the Dravidian scripts and an optional Brahmic script-sharing variant for cross-lingual subword transfer.

Per-language targets, anchored to measured Sarvam-30B (262K vocabulary, FLORES-200); worst-case capped at 2.5, floor 2.8:

Vocabulary: we will set the vocabulary at 256K (matching Gemma-3, SUTRA, and Sarvam-30B). At a model width near 6,144 the tied embedding is about 1.6B parameters, roughly 4% of the model. The final size will be fixed by the proxy sweep, since past ~256K fertility gains flatten while rare tokens are under-trained. Indicative allocation: ~95K for Latin, code, math, and symbols; ~150K across the nine Indic scripts, weighted toward Dravidian; ~11K reserved.

Metrics tracked alongside fertility

  • Parity — ratio of highest to lowest per-language fertility (max/min); closer to 1 is fairer.
  • Perplexity — for corpus quality filtering.
  • Type-token ratio and unigram entropy — for translationese detection.
  • Deduplication overlap — Jaccard on MinHash signatures.
  • Bits-per-byte and held-out cross-entropy — the tokenizer-independent capability measure used to match arms in the experiment.
